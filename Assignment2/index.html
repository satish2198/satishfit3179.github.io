Coronavirus COVID-19 Global Statistics - (January 2020 - August 2020)

This dashboard is presented in a series of visualisations for detecting potential new cluster of the Coronavirus in specific regions and Country. Readers can also see the data which shows of various correlation and different attributes related to the Covid-19 pandemic which is measured over a period of time.This allows people to view and understand which Country or Region has a good or bad record in dealing with this global pandemic.Moreover,this data is also crucial for civilains in the respected countries as it offer similar windows into how the coronavirus is spreading within their borders. Henceforth,this helps policymakers and citizens determine how to go about daily life, while reducing risks and providing researchers with a wealth of data to analyse the virus and try to curb the damage.

The darker colour demonstrates the countries with the highest total cases per 1M population. As can be seen, even though the virus orginated from China, it is countries in North and South America that are suffering largely from this pandemic. From hovering around the map, we can see that Greenland appears to be one of the Countries that as of August 2020 had 0 active covid cases.

This graph shows the Top 10 Countries which has a high DeathRate/100 Cases and RecoveredRate/100 Cases in the 6 WHO Regions! The size of the circle relates to the no of deaths/100 Cases in that specific country and the higher the value, the bigger the circle. The data is categorised as a general top 10 and top 10 in each of the WHO Region as well.

This bar graph shows the Covid-19 tests done in each of the WHO region. It also includes Countries that are not recognised by WHO. This statistic aims to accentuate how many tests are being done in correlation to the no of Cases confirmed in each region.From the chart,it shows that Europe is the highest in tests performed and South East Asia is the lowest.

This line chart shows the monthly death confirmed in millions for each region. This data is measured across from January 2020 untill start of August 2020. We can observe that the Americas WHO region peaks to the highest after May surpassing Europe. Thus, we can conclude from the graph before that even though the America regions have done lesser testing compared to Europe, it still has a larger amount of deaths and confirmed cases.

This bar graph shows the Covid-19 Outbreak by Country in a ranked order. It shows top 10 countries sorted by total deaths recorder per 100 cases. This statistic teaches us that not necessarily a country that has high number of cases can have a high mortality rate. We need to view it in terms of their population and record the number to compare it against other countries. From that observation, we can conclude that the country Chad from central Africa has the highest death rate in the world as of August 2020.

This final map shows the RecoveryRate per 100 Cases throughout the continent. The darker color depics country with a high recovery rate and it is obvious that Greenland should be one of the highestin recovery rate due to their low active cases we saw in the first map. The countries can be viewed with their total confirmed and recovered case as a whole as well.
